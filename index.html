How It Works

During a video call, the tool analyzes facial expressions, body language, tone of voice, pace of speech, and word choice in real time — then flags contradictions and shifts that signal what someone really means.

"They said 'sounds good' but their tone sounded hesitant and they looked away. They might have concerns they haven't voiced."
"Their voice went up in pitch and they're leaning back. This might signal doubt or discomfort."
"They said 'I'm fine' but their jaw tightened and they broke eye contact. They might be upset."

From there, you decide what to do. The tool gives you the signal — you own the response. Instead of replaying conversations later wondering what you missed, you catch these moments in real time and respond with confidence.
